Introduction

Welcome to JoJo's Bizarre Adventure, where the stories are so bizarre and awesome at the same time! JoJo is full of fun stories, but at the same time it makes a good job balancing the fun and weirdness with emotional moments. JoJo is the most original anime with a lot of references to real world. One of the most unique side of JoJo is also it's awesome artstyle.

Manga

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure is a Japanese manga written and illustrated by Hirohiko Araki. It was originally serialized in Shueisha's shonen manga magazine Weekly Shonen Jump from 1987 to 2004. It was then transferred to the monthly seinen manga magazine Ultra Jump in 2005. So far JoJo is divided into 9 story arc, each following a new protagonist (that's what makes this anime so unique) bearing the nickname JoJo. As of August 2023, JoJo's Bizarre Adventure has collected an incredible amount of 132 volumes.

❮ ❯

There is an old anime version known as OVA made by Studio A.P.P.P., this adaptation is a 13-episode long of part 3 which aired from 1993 - 1994.

In 2012, Araki announced that an anime adaptation was in production by David Production. Since then we got an anime adaptation of the first 6 parts of JoJo.

Manga Author

Behind the work of one of the most unique fiction stories is no other than Hirohiko Araki. Araki was born in 1960 and is, as you could guess it, a manga artist. He is best known for his long-running series JoJo's Bizarre Adventure. JoJo's began its publication since 1987 and has sold over 120 million copies, making it one of the best-selling manga series in history.

His first work was before graduating, a one-shot Poker Under Arms. Right After this, he debuted with his most famous work JoJo's. He then made a lot of spin-offs based on the JoJo series. The most famous one is about Rohan Kishibe, one of the characters in the series.

Story

If I could summarize JoJo's Bizarre Adventure's story, I would say "original". There is no other anime like JoJo's.

What is so original about JoJo's you may ask, well - there are multiple main characters. In each part, there is a new protagonist. That doesn't mean that the parts aren't connected - they are. At first, I was worried, you get fond of a character and then you realize that the new part will have a new protagonist. But trust me - that's what makes JoJo so special.

Each part has its own storyline. But it all starts of in late 19th century, when Jonathan Joestar, a young son of a wealthy landowner, meets his new adopted brother Dio Brando. This single moment led to some of the most amazing storylines and beatiful emotional moments.

Power System

JoJo's has one of the most original powersystems in all of anime, it is also one of the best and most fun I've ever seen. In the first 2 Parts, they use something called Hamon. But in later parts, they fight using something called STANDS. Stand is a physical manifestation of a person's life energy.

A Stand is an entity psychically generated by its owner, referred to as a STAND USER. The abilities and its strength all depend on the owner of the Stand. Normally, Stands aren't visible to non-Stand users. Here is the list of some Stands I chose to show you.

Star PLatinum

Star Platinum is the Stand of Jotaro Kujo. It's debut was in the Part 3 -Stardust Crusaders. Star Platinum is a close-range Stand with exceptional strength and speed. It also gained the ability to stop time.

Abilities
  • Speed
  • Strength
  • Precision
  • Stopping Time

Crazy Diamond

Crazy Diamond is the Stand of Josuke Higashikata, featured in the Part 4 - Diamond is Unbreakable. It's a powerfull Stand comparable to Star PLatinum. It possesses the ability to heal and repair objects and people.

Abilities
  • Super Strength
  • Super Speed
  • Precision
  • Restoration

Killer Queen

Killer Queen is the Stand of Yoshikage Kira, featured in Part 4 - Diamon is Unbreakable. Killer Queen is a humanoid Stand with cat-like features. Its abilities resolve around bombs, whose blasts are capable of atomizing people.

Abilities
  • Above average speed and strength
  • Bombs

Sticky Fingers

Sticky Fingers is the Stand of Bruno Bucciarati. It's first and only appereance was in Part 5 - Golden Wind. Sticky Fingers is a short-range humanoid Stand that can create zippers on any surface.

Abilities
  • Strength
  • Speed
  • Creating zippers on any surface

Stone Free

Stone Free is the Stand of Jolyne Cujoh. It is featured in the Part 6 - Stone Ocean. Stone Free usually manifests as Jolyne's ability to unravel into a string, but ultimately has a base form of a powerful close-range humanoid Stand.

Abilities
  • Strength
  • Speed
  • Strings - versatility
  • Integrated abilities

Joseph Joestar

First Appearance: Part 2 - Battle Tendency
Nationality: British-American
Stand: Hermit Purple

Joseph Joestar is the main protagonist of the second part of JoJo's Bizarre Adventure series, Battle Tendency, and the second JoJo of the series. He also appears as a primary ally in Stardust Crusaders (Part 3) and a secondary ally in Diamond is Unbreakable (Part 4).

Joseph is a natural-born Ripple user and eventual Stand user, wielding the psychic photographic Stand, Hermit Purple. An exuberant troublemaker with an exceptional talent for trickery, Joseph meets the fantastic threats approaching him throughout his life with initiative and impressive ingenuity, battling Vampires, the Pillar Men, and malevolent Stand users.

Voice actor: Tomokazu Sugita

(Source: MAL)

Jotaro Kujo

First Appearance: Part 3 - Stardust Crusaders
Nationality: Japanese
Stand: Star Platinum

Joutarou is Holy Kuujou's son and Joseph Joestar's grandson. When Joutarou first appears, he turned himself in to the police because he believes his Stand is an evil spirit possessing him. To demonstrate, he steals a gun from a prison guard, and tries to shoot himself in the head—a phantom hand emerges from his arm and catches the bullet, however, only Holy sees the hand. This act naturally earned the widespread fear of the guards and other prisoners towards him.

Joutarou is both fierce and gentle, and he shows these traits in various scenes, from the fight with N'Dour to the encounter with D'Arby. One shining example is how he spared Noriaki Kakyouin, instead of destroying him outright, knowing that Kakyouin attacked him unwillingly and was under Dio's influence. The same happened for Polnareff, whom he saved as well. Both became invaluable allies to the end. Unlike with both D'Arby brothers, who got the full brunt of Joutarou's ferocity, he saw in N'Dour blind loyalty rather than greed and evil that was evident with the other enemies. Though still loyal to Dio, N'Dour respected Joutarou for his strength, despite being an enemy, until he breathed his last. In return, Joutarou gave N'Dour a decent burial in the desert.

He is regarded by many as the most charismatic character of the JoJo series.

Voice actor: Daisuke Ono

(Souce: MAL)

Josuke Higashikata

First Appearance: Part 4 - Diamon is Unbreakable
Nationality: Japanese - British
Stand: Crazy Diamond

Jousuke makes his entrance into the JoJo universe as a caring, popular, and slightly naïve teenager. Inspired by his police officer grandfather's (on his mother's side) sense of justice, he isn't afraid to fight for those in need. He is confronted by Joutarou with the need to eradicate evil Stand users from his hometown, Morioh. He refuses at first, believing that it is not any of his business. However, he quickly changes his mind when his policeman grandfather is killed by a criminal Stand user. With a vow paralleling that of Peter Parker, Jousuke decides to deliver justice to evil Stand users in his grandfather's place.

Voice actor: Yuuki Ono

(Source: MAL)

Jolyne Kuujou

First Appearance: Part 6 - Stone Ocean
Nationality: Japanese - American - Italian - British
Stand: Stone Free

Quick to anger and snappy with her elders, it seemed like Jolyne was a lost cause. She was irresponsible and flighty, at first only looking to satisfy a cheap thrill. Bitter at Joutarou for leaving the family for so long, she blamed him for her misguided outlook. However, she still had honorable morales and treasured her mother. She would selflessly do everything in her power to help her friends. Jolyne believed that her loved ones had a good heart and an honest nature. Unfortunately, she can believe in them to the point of being gullible as it was through Romeo's lies that she was sentenced to a prison sentence of 15 years.

Voice actor: Ai Fairouz

(Source: MAL)
